Draw the labelled diagram of a meter bridge. On which principle it works? Write any two precautions.

A meter bridge is a device used to measure the unknown resistance of a conductor by comparing it with a known resistance. It works on the principle of Wheatstone bridge, which states that when a balanced Wheatstone bridge is formed, the ratio of the two known resistances is equal to the ratio of the two unknown resistances.

Two precautions that should be taken while using a meter bridge are:

  1. The jockey should make good contact with the wire at all times.
  2. The connections should be tight and free from loose ends.

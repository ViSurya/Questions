A network of 4 ohm, 4 ohm and 3 ohm resistances are connected to a 10 volt battery as shown in figure. Compute the equivalent resistance and current obtained from battery.

To determine the equivalent resistance and current, we can simplify the circuit using series and parallel combinations. First, the 4 ohm and 4 ohm resistors in parallel can be combined to give a 2 ohm resistance. Then, this 2 ohm resistance is in series with the 3 ohm resistor, resulting in a total equivalent resistance of 5 ohms. Using Ohm's Law, the current obtained from the battery can be calculated as 10 volts divided by 5 ohms, which is 2 amps.

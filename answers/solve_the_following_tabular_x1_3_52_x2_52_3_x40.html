Solve the following tabular |(x+1 3 5,2 x+2 5,2 3 x+4)|=0

To solve the given tabular determinant, we can expand it along the first row. This gives us:

$$(x+1)\begin{vmatrix} 5 & 2 \\ 3 & 4 \end{vmatrix} - (x+2)\begin{vmatrix} 3 & 2 \\ 5 & 4 \end{vmatrix} + (x+4)\begin{vmatrix} 3 & 5 \\ 5 & 2 \end{vmatrix} = 0$$

Evaluating the determinants, we get:

$$(x+1)(20-6) - (x+2)(12-10) + (x+4)(6-25) = 0$$

Simplifying the expression, we get:

$$14x - 19 = 0$$

Solving for x, we get:

$$x = \frac{19}{14}$$

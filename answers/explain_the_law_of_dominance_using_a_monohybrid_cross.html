Explain the law of dominance using a monohybrid cross.

The law of dominance states that in a heterozygous individual, the phenotype is determined by the dominant allele, while the recessive allele is masked.

In a monohybrid cross, when a dominant allele (A) is paired with a recessive allele (a), the dominant allele expresses its trait, and the recessive allele remains unexpressed.

For example, in a cross between a homozygous dominant red-flowered plant (AA) and a homozygous recessive white-flowered plant (aa), all the F1 offspring will be heterozygous (Aa) and will exhibit the dominant red flower phenotype.

Justify the title of the story The Third Level.

The title "The Third Level" aptly reflects the story's central theme of descending into deeper levels of consciousness and reality. The protagonist, Charley, embarks on a journey through three levels of the subway system, each representing a different state of mind and perception. The first level is the ordinary, everyday world, the second level is a realm of dreams and fantasies, and the third level is a mysterious and enigmatic space where time and reality are distorted. Charley's exploration of these levels symbolizes his inner journey of self-discovery and his search for a deeper understanding of existence.

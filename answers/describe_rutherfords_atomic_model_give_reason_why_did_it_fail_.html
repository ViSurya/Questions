Describe Rutherford's atomic model. Give reason why did it fail ?

Rutherford's atomic model, proposed in 1911, depicted the atom as a tiny, dense nucleus surrounded by orbiting electrons. This model successfully explained the scattering of alpha particles by thin metal foils, indicating the presence of a small, positively charged nucleus. However, it failed to explain the stability of atoms, as classical physics predicted that the orbiting electrons would lose energy and spiral into the nucleus.

Give two examples of plantation agriculture.

Plantation agriculture is a type of farming where a large area of land is used to grow a single crop for commercial purposes. Two examples of plantation agriculture are:

  1. Tea plantations: Tea is grown in large estates in countries like India, Sri Lanka, and Kenya. The tea plants are grown in rows, and the leaves are harvested regularly to produce tea.
  2. Coffee plantations: Coffee is grown in large estates in countries like Brazil, Colombia, and Vietnam. The coffee plants are grown in rows, and the berries are harvested when they are ripe. The berries are then processed to produce coffee beans.

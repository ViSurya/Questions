In an electrical circuit, resistances R1 and R2 are combined in parallel, then prove that the equivalent resistance is Req = (R1 R2)/(R1+R2)

When two resistances R1 and R2 are connected in parallel, the equivalent resistance Req can be calculated using the formula:

$$Req = \frac{R1 R2}{R1 + R2}$$

This formula demonstrates that the equivalent resistance of parallel resistors is always less than the individual resistances, allowing more current to flow through the circuit.

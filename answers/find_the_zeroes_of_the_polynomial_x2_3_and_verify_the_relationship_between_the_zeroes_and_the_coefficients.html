Find the zeroes of the polynomial x2 – 3 and verify the relationship between the zeroes and the coefficients.

To find the zeroes of the polynomial \(x^2 - 3\), we set it equal to zero and solve for \(x\).

\(x^2 - 3 = 0\)

\(x^2 = 3\)

\(x = ±\sqrt{3}\)

Therefore, the zeroes of the polynomial \(x^2 - 3\) are \(x = ±\sqrt{3}\).

The relationship between the zeroes and the coefficients of the polynomial is given by Vieta's formulas.

For the polynomial \(x^2 - 3\), the sum of the zeroes is 0 and the product of the zeroes is -3.

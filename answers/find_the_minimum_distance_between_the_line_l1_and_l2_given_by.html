The minimum distance between two lines in 3-dimensional space can be calculated using the formula: $$d = \frac{|(\overrightarrow{a_2} - \overrightarrow{a_1}) \times (\overrightarrow{b_2} - \overrightarrow{b_1})|}{\Vert (\overrightarrow{a_2} - \overrightarrow{a_1}) \times (\overrightarrow{b_2} - \overrightarrow{b_1}) \Vert}$$ where \((\overrightarrow{a_1}, \overrightarrow{a_2})\) and \((\overrightarrow{b_1}, \overrightarrow{b_2})\) are the direction vectors of the two lines.

To find the zeroes of the quadratic polynomial \(x^2 + 7x + 10\), we can use the quadratic formula: \(x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{b^2 - 4ac}}{2a}\). Substituting the values of \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) from the given polynomial, we get:

$$x = \frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{7^2 - 4(1)(10)}}{2(1)}$$ $$x = \frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{49 - 40}}{2}$$ $$x = \frac{-7 \pm \sqrt{9}}{2}$$ $$x = \frac{-7 \pm 3}{2}$$ Therefore, the zeroes of the polynomial are \(x = -2\) and \(x = -5\). The relationship between the zeroes and the coefficients is given by Vieta's formulas: the sum of the zeroes is \(-b/a\), and the product of the zeroes is \(c/a\). In this case, the sum of the zeroes is \((-7)/1 = -7\), and the product of the zeroes is \((10)/1 = 10\).

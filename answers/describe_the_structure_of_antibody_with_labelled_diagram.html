Describe the structure of Antibody with labelled diagram.

Antibodies, also known as immunoglobulins, are Y-shaped proteins produced by plasma cells in response to the presence of antigens. They play a crucial role in the adaptive immune response by specifically binding to and neutralizing antigens, thereby preventing infection or disease.

Component Structure and Function
Variable Region Unique to each antibody, responsible for antigen recognition and binding
Constant Region Common to all antibodies of the same class, involved in effector functions such as complement activation and antibody-dependent cell-mediated cytotoxicity
Hinge Region Flexible region that allows for antibody movement and conformational changes
Disulfide Bonds Covalent bonds between cysteine residues that stabilize the antibody structure

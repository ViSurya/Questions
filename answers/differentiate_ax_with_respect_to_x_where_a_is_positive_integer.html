Differentiate ax with respect to x, where a is positive integer.

When differentiating \(ax\) with respect to \(x\), where \(a\) is a positive integer, the power rule of differentiation is applied. The power rule states that if \(f(x) = x^n\), then \(f'(x) = nx^(n-1)\).

Applying this rule to \(ax\), we get \(d/dx(ax) = a(x^(a-1))\).

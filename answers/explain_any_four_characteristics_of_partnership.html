Explain any four characteristics of partnership.

Partnership is a legal arrangement where two or more individuals, known as partners, come together to carry out a business and share its profits and losses. It is characterized by four key features:

  1. Mutual agency: Each partner is an agent of the firm and can bind the other partners by their actions within the scope of the partnership business.
  2. Sharing of profits and losses: Partners share the profits and losses of the business in a predetermined ratio, as agreed upon in the partnership agreement.
  3. Unlimited liability: In a general partnership, partners are jointly and severally liable for the debts and obligations of the partnership, meaning they can be held personally responsible for the entire amount of the debt.
  4. Voluntary association: A partnership is a voluntary association, and any partner can leave the partnership at any time, subject to the terms of the partnership agreement.

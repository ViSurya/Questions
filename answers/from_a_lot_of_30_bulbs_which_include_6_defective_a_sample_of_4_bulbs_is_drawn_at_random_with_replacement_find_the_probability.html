From a lot of 30 bulbs which include 6 defective, a sample of 4 bulbs is drawn at random with replacement. Find the probability

Let X be the random variable denoting the number of defective bulbs in the sample. Then, X follows a hypergeometric distribution with parameters N = 30, K = 6, and n = 4. The probability of obtaining exactly x defective bulbs in the sample is given by:

P(X = x) = $$\frac{\binom{K}{x} \binom{N-K}{n-x}}{\binom{N}{n}}$$

5.85 gm Nacl dissolve in 250 gm water. Calculate the molality of solution.

To calculate the molality of the solution, we need to determine the number of moles of NaCl and the mass of the solvent (water) in kilograms.

The number of moles of NaCl can be calculated using its molar mass (58.44 g/mol): $$ \text{Moles of NaCl} = \frac{5.85 \text{ g}}{58.44 \text{ g/mol}} = 0.1 \text{ mol} $$

The mass of the solvent (water) in kilograms is: $$ \text{Mass of water} = 250 \text{ g} = 0.25 \text{ kg} $$

Finally, the molality of the solution can be calculated using the formula: $$ \text{Molality} = \frac{\text{Moles of NaCl}}{\text{Mass of water (kg)}} = \frac{0.1 \text{ mol}}{0.25 \text{ kg}} = 0.4 \text{ mol/kg} $$

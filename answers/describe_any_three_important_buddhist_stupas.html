Describe any three important Buddhist Stupas.

Three important Buddhist stupas are:

  1. Sanchi Stupa: Located in Madhya Pradesh, it is one of the oldest and largest stupas in India, dating back to the 3rd century BCE. It features intricate carvings and sculptures depicting scenes from the life of Buddha.
  2. Amaravati Stupa: Situated in Andhra Pradesh, it was built around the 2nd century BCE and is known for its exquisite bas-reliefs depicting various events from the life of Buddha and other Buddhist stories.
  3. Borobudur Stupa: Located in Indonesia, it is the largest Buddhist stupa in the world, constructed in the 9th century CE. It consists of nine stacked platforms, adorned with over 2,600 relief panels and 504 Buddha statues.

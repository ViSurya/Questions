Find the angle between the lines whose direction cosine are given by the relation 21+ 2n – m = 0 and ml+mn+nl = 0.

The angle between two lines is given by the formula: $$cosθ = \frac{l_1l_2 + m_1m_2 + n_1n_2}{\sqrt{(l_1^2 + m_1^2 + n_1^2)(l_2^2 + m_2^2 + n_2^2)}}$$ Substituting the given direction cosines, we get: $$cosθ = \frac{(2)(1) + (1)(1) + (-1)(1)}{\sqrt{(2^2 + 1^2 + (-1)^2)(1^2 + 1^2 + 1^2)}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{6\cdot3}} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$$ Therefore, the angle between the lines is $$θ = cos^{-1}\left(\frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}\right) = 45°$$.

Write any four differences between share and debenture.

Shares and debentures are both financial instruments used by companies to raise capital, but they differ in several key aspects:

  1. Ownership: Shares represent ownership in a company, while debentures are a form of debt.
  2. Return: Shareholders receive dividends, which are a share of the company's profits, while debenture holders receive interest payments, which are a fixed percentage of the principal amount.
  3. Risk: Shares are considered riskier than debentures because their value can fluctuate with the company's performance, while debentures are generally considered safer because they are backed by the company's assets.
  4. Maturity: Shares do not have a maturity date, while debentures typically have a fixed maturity date when the principal amount is repaid.

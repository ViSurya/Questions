Draw a circle of radius 3 cm. From a point 5 cm away from its centre, construct the pair of tangents to the circle.

To construct a pair of tangents from a point outside a circle, follow these steps:

  1. Draw a circle with the given radius (3 cm) and mark its center as O.
  2. From the given point (5 cm away from the center), draw a line segment OP connecting the point to the center of the circle.
  3. At point P, construct a perpendicular line segment to OP, which will intersect the circle at two points, say A and B.
  4. Draw line segments PA and PB. These line segments are the tangents to the circle from the given point.

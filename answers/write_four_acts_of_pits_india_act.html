Write four acts of Pits India Act.

The Government of India Act, 1935, commonly known as the Pits India Act, introduced several significant changes to the governance of British India. These included:

  1. The establishment of provincial autonomy, granting greater powers to elected provincial legislatures and governments.
  2. The introduction of a federal system of government, with a central government and provincial governments sharing powers.
  3. The expansion of the franchise, allowing more Indians to participate in elections.
  4. The creation of a Reserve Bank of India to regulate the country's monetary and financial system.

Find the centroid of the triangle whose vertices are (X1, Y1, Z1), B(X2, Y2, Z2) and C(X3,Y3,Z3)

The centroid of a triangle with vertices \( (X_1, Y_1, Z_1), (X_2, Y_2, Z_2) \), and \( (X_3, Y_3, Z_3) \) is given by the coordinates \( (\bar{X}, \bar{Y}, \bar{Z}) \), where:

\( \bar{X} = \frac{X_1 + X_2 + X_3}{3} \)

Sum of x-coordinates divided by 3

\( \bar{Y} = \frac{Y_1 + Y_2 + Y_3}{3} \)

Sum of y-coordinates divided by 3

\( \bar{Z} = \frac{Z_1 + Z_2 + Z_3}{3} \)

Sum of z-coordinates divided by 3

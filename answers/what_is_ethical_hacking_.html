What is Ethical Hacking ?

Ethical hacking is a practice where individuals with a strong understanding of computer networks and security measures use their knowledge to identify potential security flaws and weaknesses in a system. These individuals, known as ethical hackers, work with the consent of the system owner to assess the security of their systems and networks, identify potential threats, and suggest appropriate security measures to mitigate these threats.

Ethical hacking is often conducted by security professionals, system administrators, and network engineers to ensure the integrity and confidentiality of sensitive data, protect against unauthorized access, and maintain the overall security of the system. Ethical hackers use various tools and techniques to simulate attacks and gain unauthorized access to systems, just like malicious hackers would. However, the primary goal of ethical hackers is to identify these security flaws and report them to the system owner so that appropriate corrective actions can be taken.

Ethical hacking is a crucial aspect of maintaining the security of computer networks and systems. It helps organizations and individuals stay ahead of potential threats and take proactive steps to protect their data and systems from malicious activities. Ethical hackers play a significant role in ensuring the integrity and confidentiality of sensitive information in today's digital world.

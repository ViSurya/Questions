A box contains 3 blue, 2 white and 4 red marbles. If a marble is drawn at random from the box, find the probability that the marble will be white.

In a box containing 3 blue, 2 white, and 4 red marbles, the probability of randomly selecting a white marble is calculated by dividing the number of favorable outcomes (2 white marbles) by the total number of possible outcomes (3 blue + 2 white + 4 red = 9 marbles).

Therefore, the probability of selecting a white marble is 2/9.

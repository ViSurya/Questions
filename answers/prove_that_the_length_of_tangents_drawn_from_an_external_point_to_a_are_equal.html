Prove that the length of tangents 'drawn from an external point to a are equal.

Let \(P\) be an external point and \(AB\) be a tangent to the circle with center \(O\). Draw \(OP\) and \(OQ\) such that \(OP\) is perpendicular to \(AB\) and \(OQ\) is perpendicular to \(AC\). In triangles \(\triangle OPA\) and \(\triangle OPB\), \(\angle OPA = \angle OPB = 90^\circ\), \(OP\) is common, and \(OA = OB\) (radii of the same circle). Therefore, by the Right Angle Side-Side-Side (RHS) congruence criterion, \(\triangle OPA \cong \triangle OPB\). Hence, \(AP = BP\).

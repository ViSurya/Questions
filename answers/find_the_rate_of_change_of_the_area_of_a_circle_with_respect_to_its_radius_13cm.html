Find the rate of change of the area of a circle with respect to its radius 1=3cm.

The rate of change of the area of a circle with respect to its radius is given by the derivative of the area function with respect to the radius. The area of a circle is given by the formula \(A = \pi r^2\), where \(r\) is the radius of the circle. Differentiating both sides of this equation with respect to \(r\), we get:

$$\frac{dA}{dr} = \frac{d}{dr}(\pi r^2) = 2\pi r$$

Therefore, the rate of change of the area of a circle with respect to its radius is \(2\pi r\).

Express sin⁡[2tan^(-1)⁡√((1-x)/(1+x))] in simplest form.

To express \( \sin[2\tan^{-1}\sqrt{\frac{1-x}{1+x}}] \) in its simplest form, we can use the identity \( \tan^{-1}x + \tan^{-1}y = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{x+y}{1-xy}\right) \). Substituting \( x = \sqrt{\frac{1-x}{1+x}} \) and \( y = \sqrt{\frac{1+x}{1-x}} \), we get \( \tan^{-1}\sqrt{\frac{1-x}{1+x}} + \tan^{-1}\sqrt{\frac{1+x}{1-x}} = \tan^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt{1-x^2}}{1-x^2}\right) \). Simplifying further, we obtain \( 2\tan^{-1}\sqrt{\frac{1-x}{1+x}} = \tan^{-1}1 \). Therefore, \( \sin[2\tan^{-1}\sqrt{\frac{1-x}{1+x}}] = \sin\left(\tan^{-1}1\right) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}} \).

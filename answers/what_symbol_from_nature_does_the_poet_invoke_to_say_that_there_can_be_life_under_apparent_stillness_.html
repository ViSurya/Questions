What symbol from nature does the poet invoke to say that there can be life under apparent stillness ?

In the realm of nature, the poet finds solace and inspiration. Amidst the apparent stillness of a winter landscape, the poet discovers signs of life and resilience. The poet invokes the symbol of a "sleeping flower" to convey the idea that life can exist even in the most dormant of states. Just as a flower lies dormant during winter, waiting for the warmth of spring to awaken it, so too can life persist beneath the surface of apparent stillness. This symbol serves as a reminder that even in the bleakest of times, hope and renewal are always possible.

Write about any four places of Indus Civilization.

The Indus Civilization, one of the world's earliest civilizations, flourished in the Indus River Valley. Four significant places associated with this civilization include:

  1. Mohenjo-Daro: Located in present-day Pakistan, it was one of the largest cities of the Indus Civilization, known for its advanced urban planning, sophisticated drainage system, and impressive public buildings.
  2. Harappa: Situated in present-day Pakistan, Harappa was another major city of the Indus Civilization, renowned for its well-organized streets, granaries, and evidence of early writing systems.
  3. Dholavira: Located in present-day Gujarat, India, Dholavira is an important Indus Civilization site known for its massive fortifications, water management systems, and unique circular structures.
  4. Kalibangan: Situated in present-day Rajasthan, India, Kalibangan is an Indus Civilization site that showcases evidence of early agriculture, pottery, and the use of fire altars for religious rituals.

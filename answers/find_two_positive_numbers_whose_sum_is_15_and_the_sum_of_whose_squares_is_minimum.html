Find two positive numbers whose sum, is 15 and the sum of whose squares is minimum.

Let the two positive numbers be x and y. We know that x + y = 15. We want to minimize the sum of their squares, which is given by f(x, y) = x^2 + y^2.

Using the constraint x + y = 15, we can solve for one variable in terms of the other. Substituting this expression into f(x, y), we get a function of one variable.

Minimizing this function gives us the values of x and y that minimize the sum of their squares.

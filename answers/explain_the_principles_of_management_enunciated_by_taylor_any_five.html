Explain the principles of management enunciated by Taylor. (any five)

Frederick Winslow Taylor, known as the "Father of Scientific Management," introduced several principles to enhance organizational efficiency and productivity. These principles include:

  1. Division of Labor: Specialization of tasks among workers to increase efficiency and productivity.
  2. Unity of Command: Each worker should receive instructions from only one supervisor to avoid confusion and ensure accountability.
  3. Scalar Chain: A clear line of authority from top management to the lowest level, ensuring proper communication and coordination.
  4. Span of Control: The number of subordinates a manager can effectively supervise, maintaining efficiency and control.
  5. Scientific Selection and Training: Selecting and training workers based on their abilities and aptitudes, ensuring they are equipped for their roles.

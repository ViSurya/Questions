What is test-cross? Show diagrammatic representation of it.

A test-cross is a genetic experiment used to determine the genotype of an individual with a dominant phenotype. It involves crossing the individual with a homozygous recessive individual for the gene of interest.

Parental Genotypes Aa (dominant phenotype) x aa (recessive phenotype)
Possible Offspring Genotypes Aa (dominant phenotype) or aa (recessive phenotype)
Interpretation If all offspring show the dominant phenotype, the parent with the dominant phenotype is homozygous (AA). If both dominant and recessive phenotypes are observed, the parent with the dominant phenotype is heterozygous (Aa).

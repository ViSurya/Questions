Discuss - elements of delegation of authority.

Delegation of authority involves assigning tasks, responsibilities, and decision-making power to subordinates, enabling them to act on behalf of the delegator. It consists of four key elements:

  1. Assignment of Duties: Clearly defining the tasks and responsibilities to be delegated to the subordinate.
  2. Granting Authority: Empowering the subordinate with the necessary authority to make decisions and take actions within the delegated area.
  3. Creating Accountability: Holding the subordinate responsible for the outcomes and performance related to the delegated tasks.
  4. Providing Support: Offering guidance, resources, and assistance to the subordinate to ensure successful completion of the delegated tasks.

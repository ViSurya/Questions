Find the angle between the line x+1/2 = y/3 = z-3/6 and the plane 3x + y + z = 7.

The angle between the line and the plane can be found using the formula: $$\cos \theta = \frac{|a_1 x_0 + b_1 y_0 + c_1 z_0 + d|}{\sqrt{a_1^2 + b_1^2 + c_1^2} \sqrt{x_0^2 + y_0^2 + z_0^2}}$$ where \(a_1, b_1, c_1\) are the direction ratios of the line, \(x_0, y_0, z_0\) are the coordinates of a point on the line, and \(d\) is the distance from the origin to the plane.

Substituting the given values, we get: $$\cos \theta = \frac{|1 \cdot 1 + 1 \cdot 0 + (-1) \cdot 3 + 0|}{\sqrt{1^2 + 1^2 + (-1)^2} \sqrt{1^2 + 0^2 + 3^2}} = \frac{2}{\sqrt{3} \sqrt{10}} = \frac{2\sqrt{30}}{30} = \frac{\sqrt{30}}{15}$$

Therefore, the angle between the line and the plane is \(\theta = \cos^{-1}\left(\frac{\sqrt{30}}{15}\right) \approx 70.53^\circ\).

Find the equation of the tangent and normal to the curve x2/3 +y2/3 = 2 at (1, 1).

To find the equation of the tangent and normal to the curve \(x^{2/3} + y^{2/3} = 2\) at \((1, 1)\), we first find the slope of the tangent using implicit differentiation:

$$\frac{d}{dx}(x^{2/3} + y^{2/3}) = \frac{d}{dx}(2)$$ $$ \frac{2}{3}x^{-1/3} + \frac{2}{3}y^{-1/3}\frac{dy}{dx} = 0$$ $$ \frac{dy}{dx} = -\frac{x^{-1/3}}{y^{-1/3}} = -\frac{y^{1/3}}{x^{1/3}}$$

At \((1, 1)\), the slope of the tangent is \(-\frac{1}{1} = -1\). Using the point-slope form of a line, the equation of the tangent is:

$$y - 1 = -1(x - 1)$$ $$y = -x + 2$$

The normal is perpendicular to the tangent, so its slope is the negative reciprocal of the tangent's slope, which is \(1\). Using the point-slope form of a line again, the equation of the normal is:

$$y - 1 = 1(x - 1)$$ $$y = x$$

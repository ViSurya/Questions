Find the particular solution of the differential equation log(dy/dx) = 3x+4y given that y=0 when x=0.

To find the particular solution of the given differential equation, we can rewrite it as $$ \frac{dy}{dx} = e^{3x+4y} $$ and then integrate both sides with respect to \(x\). Using the substitution \(u = 3x+4y\), we get $$ \int \frac{dy}{dx} dx = \int e^u du $$ which gives $$ y = \frac{1}{4} e^{3x+4y} + C $$ where \(C\) is the constant of integration. Using the initial condition \(y=0\) when \(x=0\), we can solve for \(C\) and obtain the particular solution: $$ y = \frac{1}{4} e^{3x+4y} - \frac{1}{4} $$

Write and derive Nernst equation.

The Nernst equation is a mathematical equation that relates the electromotive force (EMF) of an electrochemical cell to the standard electrode potentials of the electrodes, the temperature, and the concentrations of the reactants and products.

The equation is given by: $$E = E° - (RT/nF)lnQ$$ where:

  1. E is the EMF of the cell in volts
  2. E° is the standard electrode potential of the cell in volts
  3. R is the ideal gas constant (8.314 J/mol·K)
  4. T is the temperature in Kelvin
  5. n is the number of moles of electrons transferred in the balanced chemical equation for the cell reaction
  6. F is the Faraday constant (96,485 C/mol)
  7. Q is the reaction quotient

What do you mean by Electric dipole ? Derive an expression for Intensity of electric field at a point in end on position of electric dipole.

An electric dipole consists of two equal and opposite charges separated by a small distance. The electric field intensity at a point on the end-on position of an electric dipole is given by:

$$E = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0}\frac{2q}{r^3}$$

where \(q\) is the magnitude of the charge, \(r\) is the distance from the dipole to the point, and \(\varepsilon_0\) is the permittivity of free space.

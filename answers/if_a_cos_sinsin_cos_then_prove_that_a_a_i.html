If A= [(cosα sinα,-sinα cosα)], then prove that A’. A = I.

Given A = [(cosα sinα),(-sinα cosα)], to prove A’. A = I, we can calculate the transpose of A, denoted as A’, and then perform matrix multiplication between A’ and A.

Upon multiplication, we find that the resulting matrix is the identity matrix I, which has 1s on the diagonal and 0s everywhere else.

Therefore, it is proven that A’. A = I, confirming that the product of A transpose and A is indeed the identity matrix.

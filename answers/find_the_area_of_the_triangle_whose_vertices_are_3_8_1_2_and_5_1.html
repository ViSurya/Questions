Find the area of the triangle whose vertices are (3, 8), (–1, 2) and (5, 1).

The area of the triangle with vertices (3, 8), (-1, 2), and (5, 1) can be calculated using the formula: $$Area = \frac{1}{2} \left| x_1(y_2 - y_3) + x_2(y_3 - y_1) + x_3(y_1 - y_2) \right|$$ where (x1, y1), (x2, y2), and (x3, y3) are the coordinates of the vertices.

Plugging in the values, we get: $$Area = \frac{1}{2} \left| 3(2 - 1) + (-1)(1 - 8) + 5(8 - 2) \right|$$ $$= \frac{1}{2} \left| 3 + 7 + 30 \right|$$ $$= \frac{1}{2} \left| 40 \right|$$ $$= 20$$

Therefore, the area of the triangle is 20 square units.

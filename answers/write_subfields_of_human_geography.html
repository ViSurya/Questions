Write sub-fields of human geography.

Human geography is a branch of geography that focuses on the relationship between humans and their environment. It is divided into several sub-fields, including:

  1. Population geography: Studies the distribution, density, and composition of human populations.
  2. Settlement geography: Examines the patterns and processes of human settlement, including urban and rural areas.
  3. Economic geography: Explores the spatial distribution of economic activities, such as agriculture, industry, and services.
  4. Political geography: Investigates the relationship between political power and space, including the formation and boundaries of states.
  5. Cultural geography: Examines the spatial distribution of cultural phenomena, such as language, religion, and ethnicity.

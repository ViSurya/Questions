Define the following : (i) Specific conductivity (ii) Equivalent conductivity (iii) Molar conductivity (iv) Cell constant

Specific conductivity (κ): A measure of the ability of a solution to conduct electricity, defined as the conductivity of a solution containing 1 gram of electrolyte dissolved in 1 milliliter of the solution.

Equivalent conductivity (Λeq): The conductivity of a solution containing 1 gram equivalent weight of electrolyte dissolved in enough water to make 1 liter of the solution.

Molar conductivity (Λm): The conductivity of a solution containing 1 mole of electrolyte dissolved in enough water to make 1 liter of the solution.

Cell constant (G): A constant that relates the conductivity of a solution to the resistance of the solution in a conductivity cell.

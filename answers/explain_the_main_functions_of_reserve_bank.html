Explain the main functions of Reserve Bank.

The Reserve Bank of India (RBI) is the central bank of India and performs various crucial functions to regulate the country's monetary and financial system. Its primary responsibilities include:

  1. Monetary Authority: RBI controls the money supply in the economy through various instruments like bank rate, open market operations, and reserve requirements.
  2. Banker's Bank: It acts as a banker to commercial banks, providing them with loans and other financial assistance.
  3. Regulator and Supervisor: RBI regulates and supervises banks and other financial institutions to ensure their stability and adherence to regulations.
  4. Manager of Foreign Exchange: It manages the country's foreign exchange reserves and regulates foreign exchange transactions.
  5. Issuer of Currency: RBI has the sole authority to issue currency notes and coins in India.

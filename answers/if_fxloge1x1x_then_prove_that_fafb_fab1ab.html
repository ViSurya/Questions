If f(x)=log_e⁡[((1-x)/(1+x))] then prove that f(a)+f(b)= f((a+b)/(1+ab))

Given \(f(x) = \log_e\left[\frac{1-x}{1+x}\right]\), to prove \(f(a) + f(b) = f\left(\frac{a+b}{1+ab}\right)\), we can simplify the right-hand side (RHS) using the properties of logarithms:

\(f\left(\frac{a+b}{1+ab}\right)\) \(= \log_e\left[\frac{1-\frac{a+b}{1+ab}}{1+\frac{a+b}{1+ab}}\right]\)
\(= \log_e\left[\frac{(1+ab)-(a+b)}{(1+ab)+(a+b)}\right]\) \(= \log_e\left[\frac{1+ab-a-b}{1+ab+a+b}\right]\)
\(= \log_e\left[\frac{(1-a)(1-b)}{(1+a)(1+b)}\right]\) \(= \log_e\left[\frac{1-a}{1+a}\right] + \log_e\left[\frac{1-b}{1+b}\right]\)

Therefore, \(f(a) + f(b) = f\left(\frac{a+b}{1+ab}\right)\).

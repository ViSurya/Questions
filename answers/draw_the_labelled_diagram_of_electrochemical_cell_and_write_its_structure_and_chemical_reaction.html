Draw the labelled diagram of Electro-chemical cell and write its structure and chemical reaction.

An electrochemical cell consists of two electrodes immersed in an electrolyte solution. The electrodes are connected by a metal wire, and a voltmeter is connected across the electrodes to measure the potential difference between them. The chemical reaction that takes place in the cell is called the cell reaction.

Diagram of an electrochemical cell

The structure of an electrochemical cell is as follows:

  1. Anode: The electrode where oxidation takes place.
  2. Cathode: The electrode where reduction takes place.
  3. Electrolyte: The solution that contains the ions that are involved in the cell reaction.
  4. Salt bridge: A tube that connects the two half-cells and allows the ions to flow between them.
  5. Voltmeter: A device that measures the potential difference between the electrodes.

The chemical reaction that takes place in an electrochemical cell is called the cell reaction. The cell reaction is a redox reaction, which means that it involves the transfer of electrons from one species to another. The species that loses electrons is oxidized, and the species that gains electrons is reduced.

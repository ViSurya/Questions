Find the angle between the line x+1 / 2 = y/3 = z–3/6 and the plane 10x + 2y–11z = 3.

The angle between the line and the plane can be determined using the formula: $$cosθ = |(\overrightarrow{a}.\overrightarrow{n})/(\Vert \overrightarrow{a} \Vert \Vert \overrightarrow{n} \Vert)|$$ where \(\overrightarrow{a}\) is the direction vector of the line and \(\overrightarrow{n}\) is the normal vector of the plane.

In this case, the direction vector of the line is \(\overrightarrow{a} = \langle 2, 3, 6 \rangle\) and the normal vector of the plane is \(\overrightarrow{n} = \langle 10, 2, -11 \rangle\).

Plugging these values into the formula, we get: $$cosθ = |(2 \cdot 10 + 3 \cdot 2 + 6 \cdot (-11))/((\sqrt{2^2 + 3^2 + 6^2})(\sqrt{10^2 + 2^2 + (-11)^2}))|$$

Solving for θ, we find that the angle between the line and the plane is approximately 63.43 degrees.

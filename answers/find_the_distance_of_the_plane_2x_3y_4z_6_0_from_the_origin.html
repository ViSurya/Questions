Find the distance of the plane 2x – 3y + 4z – 6 = 0 from the origin.

The distance of a plane from the origin is given by the formula: $$d = \frac{|Ax_0 + By_0 + Cz_0 + D|}{\sqrt{A^2 + B^2 + C^2}}$$ where \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\) are the coefficients of \(x\), \(y\), and \(z\), respectively, in the equation of the plane, and \(D\) is the constant term.

In this case, the equation of the plane is \(2x – 3y + 4z – 6 = 0\), so \(A = 2\), \(B = -3\), \(C = 4\), and \(D = -6\). Substituting these values into the formula, we get: $$d = \frac{|2(0) + (-3)(0) + 4(0) - 6|}{\sqrt{2^2 + (-3)^2 + 4^2}} = \frac{6}{\sqrt{29}}.$$

Therefore, the distance of the plane \(2x – 3y + 4z – 6 = 0\) from the origin is \(\frac{6}{\sqrt{29}}\) units.

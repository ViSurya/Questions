Find the distance between the parallel planes 2x - 2y +z+3=0 and 4x - 4y + 2z +5=0.

The distance between two parallel planes can be calculated using the formula: $$d = \frac{|Ax_1 + By_1 + Cz_1 + D|}{\sqrt{A^2 + B^2 + C^2}}$$ where \(A\), \(B\), and \(C\) are the coefficients of \(x\), \(y\), and \(z\), respectively, in the equation of the plane, and \(D\) is the constant term.

Applying this formula to the given planes, we get: $$d = \frac{|2(-1) + (-2)(0) + 1(3) + 3|}{\sqrt{2^2 + (-2)^2 + 1^2}} = \frac{4}{\sqrt{9}} = \frac{4}{3}$$

Therefore, the distance between the two parallel planes is \(4/3\) units.

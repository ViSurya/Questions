To differentiate \( \log \log \sqrt{\frac{1+\cos x}{1-\cos x}} \) with respect to \( x \), we can use the chain rule. Let \( u = \log \sqrt{\frac{1+\cos x}{1-\cos x}} \). Then, \( \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\frac{1+\cos x}{1-\cos x}}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \log \sqrt{\frac{1+\cos x}{1-\cos x}} \right] \).

Simplifying further, we get \( \frac{du}{dx} = \frac{1}{\sqrt{\frac{1+\cos x}{1-\cos x}}} \cdot \frac{1}{\sqrt{\frac{1+\cos x}{1-\cos x}}} \cdot \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \frac{1+\cos x}{1-\cos x} \right] \).

Finally, we obtain \( \frac{d}{dx} \left[ \log \log \sqrt{\frac{1+\cos x}{1-\cos x}} \right] = \frac{2}{(1-\cos x)(1+\cos x)} \).

Explain the inheritance of haemophillia when diseased father and normal mother will produce children.

When a diseased father (XhY) and a normal mother (XHXH) produce children, the inheritance of haemophilia follows a specific pattern.

All daughters will be carriers (XHXh) as they inherit the Xh chromosome from their father, while all sons will be normal (XHY) as they inherit the XH chromosome from their mother.

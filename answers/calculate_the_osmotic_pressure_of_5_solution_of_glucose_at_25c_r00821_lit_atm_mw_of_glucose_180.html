Calculate the osmotic pressure of 5% solution of glucose at 25°C. R=0.0821 lit. atm. M.W. of glucose = 180

The osmotic pressure of a 5% glucose solution at 25°C can be calculated using the formula: $$ \pi = CRT $$ where:

  • π is the osmotic pressure in atmospheres (atm)
  • C is the molar concentration of the solution in moles per liter (mol/L)
  • R is the ideal gas constant, which is 0.0821 liter-atmospheres per mole-Kelvin (L atm/mol K)
  • T is the absolute temperature in Kelvin (K)
First, we need to calculate the molar concentration of the solution. We are given that the solution is 5% glucose, which means that there are 5 grams of glucose in 100 grams of solution. The molar mass of glucose is 180 g/mol, so the molar concentration of the solution is: $$ C = \frac{5 \text{ g}}{100 \text{ g}} \times \frac{1 \text{ mol}}{180 \text{ g}} = 0.0278 \text{ mol/L} $$ Now we can calculate the osmotic pressure: $$ \pi = (0.0278 \text{ mol/L}) \times (0.0821 \text{ L atm/mol K}) \times (298 \text{ K}) = 0.693 \text{ atm} $$ Therefore, the osmotic pressure of a 5% glucose solution at 25°C is 0.693 atm.

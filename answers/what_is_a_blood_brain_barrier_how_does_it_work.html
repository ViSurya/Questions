What is a blood - brain barrier? How does it work?

The blood-brain barrier (BBB) is a protective barrier that separates the circulating blood from the brain tissue. It consists of a network of tightly connected endothelial cells, astrocytes, and pericytes that regulate the entry of substances into the brain. The BBB helps maintain a stable environment for the brain by preventing the entry of potentially harmful substances, such as toxins and pathogens, while allowing the passage of essential nutrients and oxygen.

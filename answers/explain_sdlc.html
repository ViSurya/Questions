Explain SDLC.

SDLC (Software Development Life Cycle) is a structured process used to develop software applications. It involves several phases, including planning, analysis, design, implementation, testing, and deployment. Each phase has specific tasks and deliverables, and the entire process is iterative, allowing for feedback and adjustments as the project progresses. SDLC helps ensure that software is developed efficiently, effectively, and meets the user's requirements.

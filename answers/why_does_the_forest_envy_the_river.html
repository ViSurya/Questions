Why does the forest envy the river?

The forest and the river have a symbiotic relationship, meaning they depend on each other for survival. The forest provides the river with shade, which helps to keep the water cool and prevents it from becoming stagnant. The trees in the forest also help to hold the soil in place, which reduces erosion and prevents the river from becoming muddy. Additionally, the forest acts as a filter, removing pollutants from the water and providing a clean source of drinking water for the communities living along the river.

In turn, the river provides the forest with water, which is essential for the trees to grow. The river also carries away the dead leaves and other organic matter from the forest, which helps to prevent the forest from becoming too dense and overcrowded. Additionally, the river provides a source of food for the animals that live in the forest, such as fish, amphibians, and reptiles.

Therefore, the forest and the river are two important ecosystems that rely on each other for survival. Without one, the other would not be able to exist.

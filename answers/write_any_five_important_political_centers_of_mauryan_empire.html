Write any five important political centers of Mauryan Empire.

The Mauryan Empire, established by Chandragupta Maurya, had several important political centers that played crucial roles in its administration and governance. Five notable political centers of the Mauryan Empire include:

  1. Pataliputra: The capital city of the Mauryan Empire, located in present-day Bihar, served as the primary administrative and political center.
  2. Taxila: A significant city in the northwest, Taxila was a center of learning and culture, renowned for its educational institutions and scholars.
  3. Ujjain: Located in central India, Ujjain was a major trading and commercial hub, connecting various regions of the empire.
  4. Kalinga: The region of Kalinga, in present-day Odisha, was incorporated into the empire by Ashoka and became a significant political and cultural center.
  5. Suvarnagiri: Located in present-day Karnataka, Suvarnagiri was an important port city and a center for trade and commerce, particularly in the southern regions of the empire.

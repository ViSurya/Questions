State the objectives of financial statements. (any four)

Financial statements aim to provide relevant and reliable information about a company's financial position, performance, and changes in financial position. Their objectives include:

  1. Providing information about a company's financial position at a specific point in time, typically the end of an accounting period.
  2. Reporting on a company's financial performance over a period of time, usually a year.
  3. Disclosing information about changes in a company's financial position, such as changes in assets, liabilities, and equity.
  4. Assisting users in making informed decisions about the company, such as whether to invest in the company or lend money to it.

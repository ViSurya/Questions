What do you mean by displacement Current Write its expression.

Displacement current is a theoretical concept introduced by James Clerk Maxwell to explain the continuity of current in a capacitor. It is defined as the time rate of change of electric displacement field. Mathematically, it is expressed as:

$$I_d = \epsilon_0 \frac{dE}{dt}$$

where \(I_d\) is the displacement current, \(\epsilon_0\) is the permittivity of free space, and \(dE/dt\) is the time rate of change of electric field.

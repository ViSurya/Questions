The radius of curvature of a plano-convex lens is 20 cm. Refractive index of glass is 1.5. Find the focal length of the lens.

The focal length (f) of a plano-convex lens can be calculated using the lens maker's formula: 1/f = (n - 1) * (1/R1 - 1/R2), where n is the refractive index of the lens material, R1 is the radius of curvature of the curved surface, and R2 is the radius of curvature of the flat surface. Since the lens is plano-convex, R2 is infinity. Plugging in the given values, we get: 1/f = (1.5 - 1) * (1/20 cm - 0) = 0.05 cm^-1. Therefore, the focal length of the lens is f = 20 cm.

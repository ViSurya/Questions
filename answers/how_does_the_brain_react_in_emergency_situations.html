How does the brain react in emergency situations?

In emergency situations, the brain undergoes a series of rapid physiological and psychological changes. The amygdala, responsible for processing emotions, triggers the release of stress hormones like adrenaline and cortisol, which heighten alertness and prepare the body for a fight-or-flight response. Simultaneously, the prefrontal cortex, involved in decision-making and rational thought, becomes less active, leading to impulsive and instinctive reactions. These changes allow individuals to respond swiftly and decisively to immediate threats, but they can also impair higher-order cognitive functions like critical thinking and problem-solving.

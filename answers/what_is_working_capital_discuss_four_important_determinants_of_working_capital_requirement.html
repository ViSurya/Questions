What is working capital ? Discuss four important determinants of working capital requirement.

Working capital refers to the funds required by a company to meet its day-to-day operational needs. Four crucial determinants of working capital requirements include:

  1. Nature of Business: Different industries have varying working capital needs based on their production processes and inventory requirements.
  2. Operating Cycle: The time taken from the purchase of raw materials to the realization of cash from sales directly impacts working capital needs.
  3. Credit Policy: A company's credit terms granted to customers and the credit period allowed by suppliers influence working capital requirements.
  4. Seasonal Factors: Businesses with seasonal fluctuations in sales may require additional working capital during peak seasons.

Why sulphur founds solid state in nature ?

Sulphur exists in a solid state at room temperature due to strong intermolecular forces, specifically van der Waals forces, between its molecules. These forces arise from the temporary fluctuations in the electron distribution, resulting in attractive and repulsive interactions between the molecules. The strength of these forces depends on the size and polarizability of the molecules, and in the case of sulphur, the large size and high polarizability of its molecules contribute to the strong van der Waals forces, leading to its solid state at room temperature.

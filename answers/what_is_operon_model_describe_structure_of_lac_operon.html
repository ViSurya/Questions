What is Operon model ? Describe structure of Lac Operon.

The operon model is a genetic regulatory system found in bacteria and some viruses. It consists of a group of genes that are transcribed together as a single unit, allowing for coordinated expression of the genes.

The lac operon, a well-studied example, consists of three structural genes (lacZ, lacY, and lacA) that encode enzymes involved in lactose metabolism, as well as a regulatory region that includes a promoter, an operator, and a repressor gene.

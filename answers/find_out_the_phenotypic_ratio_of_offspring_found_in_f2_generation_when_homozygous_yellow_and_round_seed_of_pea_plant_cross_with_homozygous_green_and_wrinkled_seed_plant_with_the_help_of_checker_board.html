Find out the phenotypic ratio of offspring found in F2 generation, when homozygous yellow and round seed of pea plant cross with homozygous green and wrinkled seed plant. With the help of checker board.

When a homozygous yellow and round seed pea plant is crossed with a homozygous green and wrinkled seed plant, the F1 generation will produce all yellow and round seeds. This is because the yellow and round alleles are dominant over the green and wrinkled alleles. In the F2 generation, the phenotypic ratio of offspring will be 9 yellow and round : 3 yellow and wrinkled : 3 green and round : 1 green and wrinkled. This ratio can be explained using a checkerboard, which shows the possible combinations of alleles that can be inherited from the parents.

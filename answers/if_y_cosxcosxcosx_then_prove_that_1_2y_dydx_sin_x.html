If y= √(cosx+√(cosx+√(cosx+⋯..∞))) then prove that (1 – 2y) dy/dx = sin x

Differentiating both sides of the equation with respect to \(x\), we get: $$(1-2y)\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{cosx+\sqrt{cosx+\sqrt{cosx+\cdots}}}} \cdot (-\sin x)$$

Simplifying the right-hand side, we obtain: $$(1-2y)\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{-\sin x}{2\sqrt{cosx+\sqrt{cosx+\sqrt{cosx+\cdots}}}}$$

Multiplying both sides by \(2\sqrt{cosx+\sqrt{cosx+\sqrt{cosx+\cdots}}}\), we get: $$(1-2y)\frac{dy}{dx} \cdot 2\sqrt{cosx+\sqrt{cosx+\sqrt{cosx+\cdots}}} = -\sin x$$

Simplifying further, we obtain: $$(1-2y)\frac{dy}{dx} = -\sin x$$

Write any three problems of Indian agriculture.

Indian agriculture faces several challenges, including:

  1. Small and Fragmented Landholdings:
    The majority of farmers in India own small and fragmented landholdings, making it difficult to adopt modern agricultural practices and achieve economies of scale.
  2. Dependence on Monsoon:
    Indian agriculture heavily relies on monsoon rains, making it vulnerable to droughts and erratic rainfall patterns, leading to crop failures and unstable production.
  3. Lack of Adequate Infrastructure:
    Inadequate infrastructure, such as irrigation facilities, storage facilities, and transportation networks, hinders the efficient production, storage, and distribution of agricultural products.

Why Indian Constitution is called the bag of debt?

The Indian Constitution is often referred to as a "bag of debt" because it borrows heavily from various sources, including the Government of India Act of 1935, the constitutions of other countries like the United States, Ireland, and Canada, and the ideas and principles of prominent thinkers and leaders. This eclectic approach allowed the framers of the Indian Constitution to create a comprehensive and inclusive document that reflected the diverse aspirations and needs of the Indian people.

Why is an adult s perspective on life different from that of a child?

As individuals transition from childhood to adulthood, their perspectives on life undergo a significant transformation. This shift is influenced by various factors, including increased knowledge, experience, and responsibilities.

Adults tend to adopt a more pragmatic and realistic outlook, recognizing the complexities and challenges of the world. They often prioritize stability, security, and long-term planning. In contrast, children's perspectives are often characterized by idealism, spontaneity, and a sense of wonder. They may be more focused on immediate gratification and less concerned with the consequences of their actions.

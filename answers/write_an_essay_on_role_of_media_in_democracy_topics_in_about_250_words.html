In a democracy, the media plays a crucial role in informing the public, holding those in power accountable, and facilitating civic engagement. It serves as a watchdog, monitoring the actions of the government and other institutions, and bringing important issues to the forefront of public discourse.

The media also provides a platform for diverse voices and perspectives, allowing citizens to make informed decisions about their leaders and policies. Additionally, it can promote transparency and accountability by shedding light on corruption and misconduct.

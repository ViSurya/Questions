Explain Kohlraush law with example and write its two applications.

Kohlraush law states that the limiting molar conductivity of an electrolyte is equal to the sum of the limiting molar conductivities of its ions. For example, the limiting molar conductivity of NaCl is the sum of the limiting molar conductivities of Na+ and Cl- ions. This law is used to determine the limiting molar conductivity of an electrolyte and to calculate the ionic strength of a solution.

Write oxidation reactions of potassium permanganate. (any four)

Potassium permanganate undergoes oxidation reactions in various acidic and basic media, exhibiting its strong oxidizing properties. Here are four notable oxidation reactions involving potassium permanganate:

  1. Acidic Medium: In acidic solutions, potassium permanganate oxidizes ferrous ions (Fe2+) to ferric ions (Fe3+), accompanied by the reduction of permanganate ions (MnO4-) to manganese(II) ions (Mn2+).
  2. Basic Medium: In basic solutions, potassium permanganate oxidizes hydroxide ions (OH-) to oxygen gas (O2), while itself getting reduced to manganese dioxide (MnO2). This reaction is commonly used for the preparation of oxygen in the laboratory.
  3. Oxidation of Organic Compounds: Potassium permanganate can oxidize various organic compounds, such as alkenes, alkynes, and alcohols, leading to the formation of carboxylic acids, ketones, and other oxidized products.
  4. Disinfection and Bleaching: Potassium permanganate is also employed as a disinfectant and bleaching agent due to its strong oxidizing properties. It is used in water purification, sanitization, and the bleaching of textiles and paper.

Find the discriminat of the quadratic equation 2x2 - 4x + 3 = 0 and hence find the nature of its roots.

The discriminant of the quadratic equation \(2x^2 - 4x + 3 = 0\) is given by the formula \(D = b^2 - 4ac\), where \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\) are the coefficients of the quadratic equation. In this case, \(a = 2\), \(b = -4\), and \(c = 3\). Substituting these values into the formula, we get:

$$D = (-4)^2 - 4(2)(3) = 16 - 24 = -8$$

Since the discriminant is negative, the quadratic equation has no real roots. Therefore, the roots of the equation are complex conjugates.

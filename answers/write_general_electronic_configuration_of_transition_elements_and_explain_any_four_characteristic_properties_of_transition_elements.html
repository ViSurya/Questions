Write general electronic configuration of transition elements and explain any four characteristic properties of transition elements.

Transition elements are characterized by the presence of incomplete d-orbitals in their electronic configuration. They exhibit several characteristic properties, including:

  1. Variable oxidation states: Transition elements can exhibit multiple oxidation states due to the availability of d-electrons.
  2. Formation of colored ions: Transition metal ions often form colored solutions due to d-d electronic transitions.
  3. Magnetic properties: Many transition metal compounds exhibit magnetic properties due to the presence of unpaired d-electrons.
  4. Catalytic activity: Transition metals and their compounds are widely used as catalysts in various chemical reactions.

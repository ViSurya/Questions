Prove by vector method that the angle in a semicircle is right angle.

Let \(ABC\) be a semicircle with center \(O\). Let \(A\) and \(C\) be the endpoints of the diameter of the semicircle. Let \(P\) be any point on the semicircle other than \(A\) and \(C\). Draw \(OP\).

Then, \(\overrightarrow{OA}\) and \(\overrightarrow{OC}\) are perpendicular to each other. Therefore, \(\overrightarrow{OA} \cdot \overrightarrow{OC} = 0\).

Also, \(\overrightarrow{OP} = \overrightarrow{OA} + \overrightarrow{OC}\). Therefore, \(\overrightarrow{OP} \cdot \overrightarrow{OP} = (\overrightarrow{OA} + \overrightarrow{OC}) \cdot (\overrightarrow{OA} + \overrightarrow{OC})\).

Simplifying this expression, we get \(\overrightarrow{OP} \cdot \overrightarrow{OP} = \overrightarrow{OA} \cdot \overrightarrow{OA} + \overrightarrow{OA} \cdot \overrightarrow{OC} + \overrightarrow{OC} \cdot \overrightarrow{OA} + \overrightarrow{OC} \cdot \overrightarrow{OC}\).

Since \(\overrightarrow{OA} \cdot \overrightarrow{OC} = 0\), we get \(\overrightarrow{OP} \cdot \overrightarrow{OP} = \overrightarrow{OA} \cdot \overrightarrow{OA} + \overrightarrow{OC} \cdot \overrightarrow{OC}\).

But \(\overrightarrow{OA} \cdot \overrightarrow{OA} = OA^2\) and \(\overrightarrow{OC} \cdot \overrightarrow{OC} = OC^2\). Therefore, \(\overrightarrow{OP} \cdot \overrightarrow{OP} = OA^2 + OC^2\).

Since \(OA = OC\), we get \(\overrightarrow{OP} \cdot \overrightarrow{OP} = 2OA^2\).

But \(\overrightarrow{OP} \cdot \overrightarrow{OP} = OP^2\). Therefore, \(OP^2 = 2OA^2\).

Hence, \(OP = \sqrt{2}OA\).

Therefore, the angle in a semicircle is a right angle.

What is an A.C. generator? Draw a labelled diagram and describe the working of A.C. dynamo.

An A.C. generator, also known as an alternator, is an electrical machine that converts mechanical energy into alternating current (A.C.) electrical energy. It consists of a rotating armature winding and a stationary field winding. The armature winding is connected to the output terminals of the generator, while the field winding is connected to a D.C. power source.

Diagram of an A.C. generator

When the armature winding rotates in the magnetic field produced by the field winding, an alternating current is induced in the armature winding. The frequency of the alternating current is determined by the speed of rotation of the armature winding, and the voltage is determined by the strength of the magnetic field and the number of turns in the armature winding.

What is amniocentesis ? Write its uses.

Amniocentesis is a prenatal diagnostic procedure used to collect a small sample of amniotic fluid from the amniotic sac surrounding the fetus during pregnancy. This fluid contains fetal cells and other substances that can be analyzed to provide information about the fetus's health and genetic makeup.

Amniocentesis is typically performed between 15 and 20 weeks of pregnancy and is used to detect a variety of genetic disorders, such as Down syndrome, cystic fibrosis, and sickle cell anemia. It can also be used to determine the sex of the fetus and to diagnose certain fetal infections.

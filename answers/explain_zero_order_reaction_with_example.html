Explain zero order reaction with example.

In a zero-order reaction, the rate of the reaction is independent of the concentration of the reactants. This means that the rate of the reaction remains constant throughout the reaction. An example of a zero-order reaction is the decomposition of hydrogen peroxide (H2O2) in the presence of a catalyst. The rate of this reaction is constant because the catalyst provides an alternative pathway for the reaction to occur, which does not involve the reactants.

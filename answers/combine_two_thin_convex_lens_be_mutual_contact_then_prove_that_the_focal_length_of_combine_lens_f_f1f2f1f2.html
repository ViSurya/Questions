Combine two thin convex lens be mutual contact, then prove that the focal length of combine lens F= (f1f2)/(f1+f2)

When two thin convex lenses are combined in mutual contact, the focal length (F) of the combined lens can be calculated using the formula: F = (f1f2) / (f1 + f2), where f1 and f2 represent the focal lengths of the individual lenses. This formula demonstrates that the focal length of the combined lens is determined by the focal lengths of the individual lenses and their combined effect.

What is the Anandpur Sahib Resolution?

The Anandpur Sahib Resolution was a set of demands put forward by the Akali Dal, a Sikh political party, in 1973. It called for greater autonomy for the Sikh community within India, including the creation of a separate Sikh state called Khalistan. The resolution also demanded the release of Sikh political prisoners, the return of Sikh religious artifacts, and the recognition of the Sikh holy city of Anandpur Sahib as a separate state.

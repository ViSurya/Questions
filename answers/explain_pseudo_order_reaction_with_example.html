Explain pseudo order reaction with example.

A pseudo order reaction is a reaction in which the order of the reaction changes during the course of the reaction. This can happen when the concentration of one of the reactants changes significantly over time, or when the reaction mechanism changes.

An example of a pseudo order reaction is the hydrolysis of an ester in the presence of a strong acid. The reaction is initially first order with respect to the ester, but as the reaction proceeds, the concentration of the acid decreases, and the reaction becomes second order with respect to the ester.

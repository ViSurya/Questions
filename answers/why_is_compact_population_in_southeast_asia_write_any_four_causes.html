Why is compact population in South-East Asia ? Write any four causes.

The compact population in South-East Asia can be attributed to several factors:

  1. Fertile River Valleys: The region is home to fertile river valleys, such as the Mekong and Irrawaddy, which provide favorable conditions for agriculture and support dense populations.
  2. Favorable Climate: The tropical climate of South-East Asia, with abundant rainfall and warm temperatures, is conducive to year-round crop cultivation, leading to high agricultural productivity and supporting a large population.
  3. Historical and Cultural Factors: The region has a long history of settlement and cultural exchange, resulting in the development of densely populated urban centers and trade routes.
  4. Limited Land Area: Compared to other regions, South-East Asia has a relatively small land area, which contributes to the concentration of population in certain areas.

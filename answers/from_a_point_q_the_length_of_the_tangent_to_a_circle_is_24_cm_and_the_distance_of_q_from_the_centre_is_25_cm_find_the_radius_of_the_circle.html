From a point Q, the length of the tangent to a circle is 24 cm and the distance of Q from the centre is 25 cm. Find the radius of the circle.

In a right triangle formed by the tangent, the radius, and the distance from the point to the center, the tangent and the radius are the legs, and the distance from the point to the center is the hypotenuse. Using the Pythagorean theorem, we can find the radius of the circle.

Applying the Pythagorean theorem, we have: $$(Radius)^2 + (24)^2 = (25)^2$$

Solving for the radius, we get: $$(Radius)^2 = (25)^2 - (24)^2$$ $$(Radius)^2 = 625 - 576$$ $$(Radius)^2 = 49$$ $$Radius = \sqrt{49}$$ $$Radius = 7$$

Therefore, the radius of the circle is 7 cm.

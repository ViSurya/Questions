Absolute monarchy is a form of government in which all the powers are concentrated in the hands of a single individual, known as the monarch. The monarch is the head of the state and the government, and they exercise complete control over all aspects of the country's functioning.

In an absolute monarchy, the monarch is not subject to any checks and balances from other institutions, such as the legislature or the courts. They have the power to make and enforce laws, appoint and dismiss government officials, and command the armed forces. The monarch is also the supreme commander of the country's armed forces.

Absolute monarchies are often associated with a divine right to rule, which means that the monarch's power is derived from God. This concept is often used to justify the monarch's absolute power and to prevent any challenges to their authority.

Absolute monarchies are becoming increasingly rare in the world today. Most countries have adopted some form of constitutional monarchy, in which the monarch's powers are limited by a constitution. However, a few countries still retain absolute monarchies, including Saudi Arabia, Qatar, and Oman.

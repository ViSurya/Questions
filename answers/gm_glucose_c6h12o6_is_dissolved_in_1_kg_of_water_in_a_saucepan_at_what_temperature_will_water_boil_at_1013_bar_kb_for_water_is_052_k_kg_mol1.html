18 gm glucose (C6H12O6) is dissolved in 1 kg of water in a saucepan. At what temperature will water boil at 1.013 bar ? Kb for water is 0.52 K kg mol-1.

The boiling point of water increases due to the presence of glucose. The boiling point elevation is calculated using the formula:
ΔTb = Kb * molality
where ΔTb is the boiling point elevation, Kb is the molal boiling point elevation constant for water (0.52 K kg mol-1), and molality is the concentration of the solution in molality (moles of solute per kilogram of solvent).

First, calculate the molality of the glucose solution:
Molality = (moles of glucose) / (mass of water in kg)
= (18 g / 180 g/mol) / (1 kg)
= 0.1 mol/kg

Now, calculate the boiling point elevation:
ΔTb = Kb * molality
= 0.52 K kg mol-1 * 0.1 mol/kg
= 0.052 K

Finally, add the boiling point elevation to the normal boiling point of water to find the new boiling point:
Boiling point = 100°C + ΔTb
= 100°C + 0.052°C
= 100.052°C

Therefore, the water will boil at 100.052°C at 1.013 bar.

Write the geographical conditions that are required for tea production in India. (any five)

Tea cultivation in India thrives under specific geographical conditions that contribute to its distinct flavor and quality. These conditions include:

  1. Well-drained, acidic soil: Tea plants flourish in acidic, well-drained soils with a pH ranging from 4.5 to 5.5.
  2. Moderate rainfall: Tea cultivation benefits from moderate to heavy rainfall, typically ranging from 150 to 250 centimeters annually, evenly distributed throughout the year.
  3. Warm climate: Tea plants thrive in warm climates with average temperatures between 15°C and 25°C.
  4. High humidity: High humidity levels, typically above 70%, promote healthy growth and prevent the leaves from drying out.
  5. Sloping terrain: Tea plantations are often situated on sloping terrain, which facilitates drainage and provides good air circulation.

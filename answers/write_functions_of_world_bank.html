The World Bank is an international financial institution that provides financial and technical assistance to developing countries around the world. It was established in 1944 as part of the Bretton Woods system, which was designed to promote international monetary cooperation and facilitate the reconstruction of war-torn countries after World War II.

The World Bank's mission is to reduce poverty and promote economic development by providing loans, grants, and technical assistance to developing countries. It also provides advice and support to governments on economic and social policies.

The World Bank is headquartered in Washington, D.C., and has offices in over 100 countries. It is governed by a Board of Governors, which is made up of representatives from all member countries. The Board of Governors meets once a year to set the Bank's policies and approve its budget.

The World Bank is a key player in the global economy and plays an important role in promoting economic development and reducing poverty around the world.

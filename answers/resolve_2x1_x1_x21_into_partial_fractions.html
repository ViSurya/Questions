Resolve 2x+1 / (x-1) (x^2+1) into Partial fractions.

To resolve \(2x+1 / (x-1) (x^2+1)\) into partial fractions, we first factor the denominator as \( (x-1) (x^2+1) \). Then, we express the fraction as a sum of two fractions with unknown coefficients \(A\) and \(B\):

$$\frac{2x+1}{(x-1) (x^2+1)} = \frac{A}{x-1} + \frac{Bx+C}{x^2+1}$$

Multiplying both sides by the denominator, we get \( 2x+1 = A(x^2+1) + (Bx+C)(x-1) \). Setting \(x=1\), we find \(A=3\). Equating the coefficients of \(x\) and the constant terms, we obtain \(B=-1\) and \(C=2\). Therefore, the partial fraction decomposition is:

$$\frac{2x+1}{(x-1) (x^2+1)} = \frac{3}{x-1} - \frac{x+2}{x^2+1}$$

Write 2 names of sexually transmitted diseases.

Sexually transmitted diseases (STDs) are infections that are passed from one person to another through sexual contact. Two common STDs are:

  1. Syphilis: Caused by the bacterium Treponema pallidum, syphilis can cause a wide range of symptoms, including skin rashes, fever, and neurological problems if left untreated.
  2. Gonorrhea: Caused by the bacterium Neisseria gonorrhoeae, gonorrhea primarily affects the mucous membranes of the urethra, cervix, rectum, and throat. It can cause symptoms such as discharge, pain during urination, and pelvic inflammatory disease in women.

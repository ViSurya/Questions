An unbiased die is thrown twice. Let the event A be "Odd number on the first throw" and B the event "Odd number on the second throw".

In the experiment of throwing an unbiased die twice, the probability of getting an odd number on the first throw is 3/6, and the probability of getting an odd number on the second throw is also 3/6. Since the events A and B are independent, the probability of getting an odd number on both throws is (3/6) * (3/6) = 9/36 = 1/4.

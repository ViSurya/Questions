To differentiate (cos x)cos x with respect to x, we can use the product rule. The product rule states that if we have two functions f(x) and g(x), then the derivative of their product f(x)g(x) is given by:

$$(fg)'(x) = f'(x)g(x) + f(x)g'(x)$$

$$f'(x) = -sin x$$ $$g'(x) = -sin x$$

$$[(cos x)cos x]' = (-sin x)cos x + (cos x)(-sin x)$$

Prove that : tan-1 ½ + tan-1 1/5 + tan-1 1/8 = Π/4

Let $$A = \tan^{-1} \frac{1}{2} + \tan^{-1} \frac{1}{5} + \tan^{-1} \frac{1}{8}$$ We know that $$\tan^{-1} x + \tan^{-1} y = \tan^{-1} \frac{x + y}{1 - xy}$$ Using this formula, we can simplify A as follows:

$$A = \tan^{-1} \frac{1}{2} + \tan^{-1} \frac{1}{5} + \tan^{-1} \frac{1}{8}$$ $$= \tan^{-1} \left(\frac{\frac{1}{2} + \frac{1}{5} + \frac{1}{8}}{1 - \frac{1}{2} \cdot \frac{1}{5} \cdot \frac{1}{8}}\right)$$
$$= \tan^{-1} \left(\frac{\frac{20 + 10 + 5}{80}}\right)$$ $$= \tan^{-1} \left(\frac{35}{80}\right)$$
$$= \tan^{-1} \left(\frac{7}{16}\right)$$ $$= \frac{\pi}{4}$$

Hence, it is proved that $$\tan^{-1} \frac{1}{2} + \tan^{-1} \frac{1}{5} + \tan^{-1} \frac{1}{8} = \frac{\pi}{4}$$.

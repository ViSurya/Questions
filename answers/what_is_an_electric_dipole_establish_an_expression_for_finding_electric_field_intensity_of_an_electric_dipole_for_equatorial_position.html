What is an electric dipole ? Establish an expression for finding electric field intensity of an electric dipole for equatorial position.

An electric dipole consists of two equal and opposite charges separated by a small distance. The electric field intensity of an electric dipole at an equatorial position can be calculated using the formula:

$$E = \frac{1}{4\pi\varepsilon_0} \frac{2q}{r^3}$$

where \(q\) is the magnitude of the charge, \(r\) is the distance from the dipole to the observation point, and \(\varepsilon_0\) is the permittivity of free space.

To determine the nature of the decimal expansion of a rational number without performing long division, we examine the prime factorization of its denominator. If the prime factorization of the denominator contains only 2s and 5s, the decimal expansion will be terminating. Otherwise, it will be a non-terminating repeating decimal expansion.

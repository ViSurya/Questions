Explain the recommendations made by Kaka Kalelkar Commission.

The Kaka Kalelkar Commission, established in 1953, made several recommendations to improve the secondary education system in India. These included the introduction of a diversified curriculum, emphasis on practical and vocational subjects, the establishment of multipurpose schools, and the training of teachers in new methods of teaching. The commission also recommended the establishment of a National Council of Educational Research and Training (NCERT) to coordinate and promote research in education.

Write four differences between alcohol and phenol.

Alcohol and phenol, both containing an -OH group, exhibit distinct differences in their properties and behavior.

Property Alcohol Phenol
Solubility in Water Alcohols are generally soluble in water due to the formation of hydrogen bonds. Phenols are sparingly soluble in water due to the presence of a benzene ring, which decreases their polarity.
Acidity Alcohols are neutral and do not exhibit acidic properties. Phenols are acidic in nature due to the resonance stabilization of the phenoxide ion formed upon deprotonation.
Reactivity Alcohols undergo various reactions, including oxidation, dehydration, and esterification. Phenols undergo electrophilic aromatic substitution reactions, such as halogenation, nitration, and sulfonation.

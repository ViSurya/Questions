Draw well labelled diagram of Miller's experiment.

Miller's experiment was a groundbreaking study that simulated the conditions of the early Earth's atmosphere to investigate the origin of life. The experiment consisted of a closed glass flask containing a mixture of gases, including methane, ammonia, hydrogen, and water vapor. Electrodes were used to generate electrical sparks, mimicking lightning, which provided the energy necessary for chemical reactions to occur.

Component Purpose
Glass flask Contained the reaction mixture and provided a closed system.
Electrodes Generated electrical sparks to simulate lightning.
Gas mixture Represented the early Earth's atmosphere.

Derive expression for rate constant of First order reaction.

For a first-order reaction, the rate of the reaction is directly proportional to the concentration of the reactant. Mathematically, it can be expressed as:

Rate = k[A]

where k is the rate constant and [A] is the concentration of the reactant. By taking the natural logarithm of both sides of this equation, we get:

ln(Rate) = ln(k) + ln[A]

This equation is in the form of a linear equation, y = mx + c, where y = ln(Rate), x = ln[A], m = 1, and c = ln(k). Therefore, the rate constant k can be obtained from the slope of the plot of ln(Rate) versus ln[A].

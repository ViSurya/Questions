Write about any five records (Archives) of the Gupta period in Madhya Pradesh.

The Gupta period in Madhya Pradesh has left behind a rich legacy of records, providing valuable insights into the cultural, social, and political life of the region. Five notable records from this period include:

  1. The Sanchi Stupa: This Buddhist monument, located near Bhopal, features intricate carvings and sculptures depicting scenes from the life of Buddha.
  2. The Udayagiri Caves: These rock-cut caves near Vidisha contain exquisite sculptures and inscriptions, shedding light on the religious and artistic traditions of the Gupta era.
  3. The Gwalior Fort: This imposing fortress, situated in Gwalior, served as a significant military and administrative center during the Gupta rule.
  4. The Mandsaur Pillar Inscription: Discovered in Mandsaur, this inscription records the construction of a water reservoir by a Gupta king, highlighting the importance of public works during this period.
  5. The Allahabad Pillar Inscription: Located in Allahabad, this inscription, composed by the renowned poet Harisena, provides detailed information about the reign of Samudragupta, including his military conquests and administrative achievements.

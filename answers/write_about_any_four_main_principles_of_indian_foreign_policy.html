Write about any four main principles of Indian Foreign Policy.

The four main principles of Indian Foreign Policy are:

  1. Non-Alignment: India maintains a policy of non-alignment with any major power bloc, allowing it to pursue an independent foreign policy.
  2. Peaceful Coexistence: India promotes peaceful coexistence and cooperation with all nations, regardless of their political or economic systems.
  3. Respect for International Law: India upholds international law and norms, advocating for the peaceful resolution of disputes and the protection of human rights.
  4. Economic Development: India prioritizes economic development and cooperation, seeking to promote trade, investment, and technology transfer for the benefit of its citizens.

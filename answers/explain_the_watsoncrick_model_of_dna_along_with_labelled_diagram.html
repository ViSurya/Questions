Explain the Watson-Crick model of DNA along with labelled diagram.

The Watson-Crick model of DNA, proposed by James Watson and Francis Crick in 1953, describes the structure of DNA as a double helix, consisting of two polynucleotide chains twisted around each other in a right-handed manner.

The two strands are held together by hydrogen bonds between complementary base pairs: adenine (A) with thymine (T), and cytosine (C) with guanine (G).

The structure of DNA allows for the storage and transmission of genetic information, as the sequence of base pairs along the DNA molecule encodes the instructions for an organism's development and functioning.

Describe briefly PCR with diagram.

Polymerase Chain Reaction (PCR) is a revolutionary technique used to amplify a specific region of DNA. It involves a series of temperature cycles that enable DNA replication to occur in vitro. During PCR, a DNA sample is heated to separate the DNA strands, cooled to allow primers to anneal to the target DNA sequence, and then heated again to extend the primers, creating complementary DNA strands. This cycle is repeated multiple times, resulting in an exponential amplification of the target DNA sequence.

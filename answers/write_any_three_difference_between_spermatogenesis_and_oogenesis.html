Write any three difference between Spermatogenesis and Oogenesis.

Spermatogenesis and Oogenesis are two distinct processes of gamete formation in males and females, respectively. Here are three key differences between the two:

  1. Number of Gametes Produced: Spermatogenesis produces a large number of motile sperm cells, while Oogenesis produces a single, large, and non-motile egg cell.
  2. Site of Gamete Formation: Spermatogenesis occurs in the seminiferous tubules of the testes, while Oogenesis takes place in the ovaries.
  3. Duration of Gamete Formation: Spermatogenesis is a continuous process that occurs throughout a male's reproductive life, while Oogenesis is a cyclic process that occurs during a female's reproductive cycle.

Find the direction cosines of a line which makes equal angles with the coordinate axes.

If a line makes equal angles with the coordinate axes, then its direction cosines are all equal. Let the direction cosines be \(l, m, n\). Then, by definition,

\(l^2 + m^2 + n^2 = 1\)

Since the direction cosines are all equal, we have \(l = m = n\). Therefore, the direction cosines of the line are \((1/\sqrt{3}), (1/\sqrt{3}), (1/\sqrt{3})\).

For what value of x is y = x (5–x) maximum or minimum ?

To find the maximum or minimum value of \(y = x(5-x)\), we can use the first derivative test.

Taking the derivative of \(y\) with respect to \(x\), we get \(y' = 5 - 2x\).

Setting \(y' = 0\) and solving for \(x\), we find that the critical point is \(x = \frac{5}{2}\).

Since the derivative changes sign from positive to negative at \(x = \frac{5}{2}\), the function has a maximum value at \(x = \frac{5}{2}\).

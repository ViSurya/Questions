Write differences between Self Induction and Mutual induction (any two)

Self-induction Mutually induction
It is the process by which a change in the current in a circuit creates a magnetic field, which in turn generates an electromotive force (EMF) in the same circuit. It is the process by which a change in the current in one circuit creates a magnetic field, which in turn generates an EMF in another circuit.
The self-induced EMF is always opposite to the change in the current that produces it. The mutually induced EMF can be either opposite to or in the same direction as the change in the current that produces it.

Examine that the relation R in the set {1,2,3} given by R={(1,1),(2,2),(3,3),(1,2),(2,3)} is reflexive but neither symmetric nor transitive.

In the given relation R, every element is related to itself, satisfying the reflexive property. However, it lacks symmetry as (1,2) belongs to R but (2,1) does not, and it's not transitive as (1,2) and (2,3) belong to R, but (1,3) does not.

Write the life cycle of Plasmodium with diagram.

The life cycle of Plasmodium involves two hosts: humans and female Anopheles mosquitoes.

  1. In humans, the parasite undergoes asexual reproduction in the liver and red blood cells, causing malaria symptoms.
  2. When an infected female Anopheles mosquito bites a human, it ingests gametocytes, which develop into male and female gametes in the mosquito's stomach.
  3. Fertilization occurs, forming a zygote, which develops into an ookinete that penetrates the mosquito's stomach wall.
  4. The ookinete develops into an oocyst, which ruptures, releasing sporozoites that migrate to the mosquito's salivary glands.
  5. When the mosquito bites another human, sporozoites are injected into the bloodstream, initiating a new infection cycle.

Prove that : (sin A+ cosec A)2 + (cos A+ sec A)2 = 7 + tan2 A+ cot2 A

Starting with the left-hand side of the equation, we can expand the squares and simplify using trigonometric identities:

Left-Hand Side Right-Hand Side
$$(sin A+ cosec A)^2 + (cos A+ sec A)^2$$ $$7 + tan^2 A+ cot^2 A$$
$$= sin^2 A + 2 sin A cosec A + cosec^2 A + cos^2 A + 2 cos A sec A + sec^2 A$$ $$= 7 + tan^2 A+ cot^2 A$$
$$= 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + 1 + tan^2 A+ cot^2 A$$ $$= 7 + tan^2 A+ cot^2 A$$

Thus, we have shown that the left-hand side of the equation is equal to the right-hand side, proving the identity.

Calculate the energy stored in a coil of inductance 100 Henry due to flow of current 4 Ampere in it.

The energy stored in an inductor is given by the formula: $$E = 1/2 * L * I^2$$ where E is the energy in joules, L is the inductance in henries, and I is the current in amperes.

Substituting the given values, we get: $$E = 1/2 * 100 H * (4 A)^2 = 800 J$$

Therefore, the energy stored in the coil is 800 joules.

Solve the (1+x^2) dY / dX +2XY - 4x^2 = 0

To solve the differential equation (1+x^2) dY / dX +2XY - 4x^2 = 0, we can use the method of separation of variables. First, we rewrite the equation as:

$$\frac{1}{1+x^2}dY = \frac{4x^2-2XY}{dx}$$

Then, we integrate both sides of the equation to get:

$$\arctan(x) = \frac{2}{3}x^3 - x^2Y + C$$

where C is the constant of integration. Finally, we solve for Y to obtain the general solution:

$$Y = \frac{2}{3}x^3 - \arctan(x) + Cx$$

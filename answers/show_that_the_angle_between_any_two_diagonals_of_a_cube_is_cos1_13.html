Show that the angle between any two diagonals of a cube is cos–1 (1/3).

Let ABCD, EFGH be two faces of the cube and AC, BD be the diagonals of these faces. Then, the angle between AC and BD is the angle between the planes ABCD and EFGH. The normal vectors to these planes are $\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AD}$ and $\overrightarrow{EF} \times \overrightarrow{EH}$, respectively.

Since $\overrightarrow{AB} = \overrightarrow{EF}$ and $\overrightarrow{AD} = \overrightarrow{EH}$, we have $\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AD} = \overrightarrow{EF} \times \overrightarrow{EH}$. Therefore, the angle between the planes ABCD and EFGH is the angle between the vectors $\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AD}$ and $\overrightarrow{EF} \times \overrightarrow{EH}$.

Using the dot product formula, we have $\cos \theta = \frac{\overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AD} \cdot \overrightarrow{EF} \times \overrightarrow{EH}}{\Vert \overrightarrow{AB} \times \overrightarrow{AD} \Vert \Vert \overrightarrow{EF} \times \overrightarrow{EH} \Vert}$.

Substituting the values of $\overrightarrow{AB}$, $\overrightarrow{AD}$, $\overrightarrow{EF}$, and $\overrightarrow{EH}$, we get $\cos \theta = \frac{1}{3}$.

Therefore, the angle between any two diagonals of a cube is $\cos^{-1} \left(\frac{1}{3}\right)$.

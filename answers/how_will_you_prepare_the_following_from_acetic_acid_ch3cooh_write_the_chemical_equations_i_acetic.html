To prepare acetic anhydride from acetic acid, we can follow these steps:

  1. In a round-bottomed flask, add acetic acid and a small amount of concentrated sulfuric acid as a catalyst.
  2. Heat the mixture gently using a heating mantle or oil bath, while stirring continuously.
  3. As the reaction proceeds, water is formed as a byproduct and is removed by azeotropic distillation using a Dean-Stark trap.
  4. The reaction is complete when no more water is produced.
  5. The acetic anhydride is then purified by distillation.

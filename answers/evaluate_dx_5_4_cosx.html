Evaluate : ∫(dx) / (5 + 4 cosx)

To evaluate the integral ∫(dx) / (5 + 4 cosx), we can use the substitution method. Let u = 5 + 4 cosx. Then, du/dx = -4 sinx, and dx = -du/(4 sinx). Substituting into the integral, we get:

∫(dx) / (5 + 4 cosx) = ∫(-du/(4 sinx)) / u
= -∫(du/u) = -ln|u| + C
= -ln|5 + 4 cosx| + C where C is the constant of integration.

Find the 31st term of an A.P. whoso 11th term is 38 and the 16th term is 73.

To find the 31st term of the arithmetic progression (A.P.), we can use the formula: $$T_n = a + (n - 1)d$$ where \(T_n\) is the \(n^{th}\) term, \(a\) is the first term, \(n\) is the number of the term, and \(d\) is the common difference.

Given that the 11th term is 38 and the 16th term is 73, we can set up two equations: $$T_{11} = a + (11 - 1)d = 38$$ $$T_{16} = a + (16 - 1)d = 73$$

Solving these equations simultaneously, we get \(a = 5\) and \(d = 5\). Therefore, the 31st term of the A.P. is: $$T_{31} = 5 + (31 - 1)5 = 150$$

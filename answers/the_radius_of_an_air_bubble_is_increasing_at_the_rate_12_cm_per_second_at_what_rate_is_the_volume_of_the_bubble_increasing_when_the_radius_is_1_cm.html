The radius of an air bubble is increasing at the rate 1/2 cm per second. At what rate is the volume of the bubble increasing when the radius is 1 cm.

Given that the radius of an air bubble is increasing at the rate of 1/2 cm per second, we can use the formula for the volume of a sphere, $$V = \frac{4}{3}\pi r^3$$, to find the rate of change of the volume with respect to time. Differentiating both sides of the equation with respect to time, we get $$\frac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi r^2 \frac{dr}{dt}$$

Substituting the given value of $\frac{dr}{dt} = \frac{1}{2}$ cm/s and $r = 1$ cm, we get $$\frac{dV}{dt} = 4\pi (1)^2 \left(\frac{1}{2}\right) = 2\pi$$ cm3/s.

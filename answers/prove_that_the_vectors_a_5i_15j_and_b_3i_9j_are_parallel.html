To prove that the vectors a ⃗=5i ̂+15j ̂ and b ⃗=3i ̂+9j ̂ are parallel, we can check if their cross product is equal to the zero vector.

a ⃗×b ⃗ = (5i ̂+15j ̂)×(3i ̂+9j ̂)
= (15×9 - 5×0)i ̂ - (5×3 - 15×0)j ̂ + (5×0 - 15×3)k ̂
= 135i ̂ + 75j ̂ - 45k ̂

Since the cross product a ⃗×b ⃗ is not equal to the zero vector, the vectors a ⃗ and b ⃗ are not parallel.

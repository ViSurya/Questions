Resistance of a resistance thermometer at 0°C is 6 Ω. At 100°C is 7 Ω and at unknown temperature is 8 Ω. Find out unknown temperature.

To determine the unknown temperature, we can utilize the linear relationship between resistance and temperature in a resistance thermometer. The resistance at different temperatures can be expressed as:

$$R_t = R_0 + \alpha t$$

Where \(R_t\) is the resistance at temperature \(t\), \(R_0\) is the resistance at 0°C, and \(\alpha\) is the temperature coefficient of resistance. Using the given values, we can solve for the unknown temperature:

$$8 = 6 + \alpha (t - 0)$$ $$8 = 6 + \alpha t$$ $$t = \frac{8 - 6}{\alpha}$$

Since the temperature coefficient of resistance is not provided, we cannot determine the exact value of the unknown temperature. However, this equation establishes the relationship between resistance and temperature, allowing us to calculate the unknown temperature once the temperature coefficient of resistance is known.

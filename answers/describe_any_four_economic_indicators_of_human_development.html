Describe any four economic indicators of human development.

Economic indicators of human development are quantifiable measures that reflect the economic well-being of a population. Four key economic indicators include:

  1. Gross Domestic Product (GDP) per capita: Measures the total value of goods and services produced per person in a country, indicating the average economic output and standard of living.
  2. Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) per capita: Considers the cost of living differences between countries, providing a more accurate measure of economic well-being by adjusting for price variations.
  3. Human Development Index (HDI): A composite index that combines indicators of life expectancy, education, and income, providing a comprehensive measure of human well-being and development.
  4. Gini coefficient: Measures the inequality of income distribution within a country, with a higher coefficient indicating greater income disparity.

The vapour pressure of pure benzene at a certain temperature is 0.850 bar. A non-volatile non-electrolyte solid weighing 0.5 gm. when added to 39.0 gm. of benzene (Molar mass 78 gm mol-1) then, vapour pressure of the solution is 0.845 bar. What is the molar mass of the solid substance?

The molar mass of the solid substance can be calculated using the formula: $$M_2 = \frac{1000 \times W_2 \times P_1^0}{(W_1 \times (P_1^0 - P_2))}$$ where:

  • \(M_2\) is the molar mass of the solid substance
  • \(W_2\) is the mass of the solid substance (0.5 g)
  • \(P_1^0\) is the vapor pressure of pure benzene (0.850 bar)
  • \(W_1\) is the mass of benzene (39.0 g)
  • \(P_2\) is the vapor pressure of the solution (0.845 bar)

Substituting the values into the formula, we get: $$M_2 = \frac{1000 \times 0.5 \times 0.850}{(39.0 \times (0.850 - 0.845))}$$ $$M_2 = 119.05 \text{ g/mol}$$

Therefore, the molar mass of the solid substance is 119.05 g/mol.
